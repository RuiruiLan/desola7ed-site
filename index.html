About

I started producing around 2019. As a teenage kid, I loved listening to Puppet, Jaron, and Porter Robinson, and found my passion for synths and distortion. I was lucky enough to be discovered by people who appreciated my amateur creations and gave my musical identity meaning. Over the years, my disorganized and procrastinating self grew reliant on a faint hope for moments of revelation, though they did not come nearly as often as the countless frustrating nights of build, delete, and repeat. At some point, I decided it was time to stop and reorient my attention toward friends, loved ones, and other things that give my life meaning. The d7 project, to me, is goals unfulfilled, expectations unmet, and a trauma factory of self-criticism. But I know it is something more, at least to my mentors, my closest friends, and those who have supported me and given me countless reasons to keep going. I therefore do not regret, for a single moment, everything I have done and sacrificed for him. I am, however, sorry for the people I have hurt, disappointed, and lost along the way.

-d7
